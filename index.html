GRAPHIC DESIGN

These are individual design projects where I used Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator and Indesign.

The spreads were made using a recipe from the NY Times cooking section. The book jacket for "The Giver" was made to represent the themes in the book. The poster for "The Governer's Ball" was an excercise to familiarize myself with indesign and to practice hierarchy. And the fox face was made to explore how to express gestures through simplified shapes.

DRAWINGS

For these drawings I was learning how to apply what we saw visually onto paper with charcoal and pencils. The first piece with the pepper was the most challenging piece I had made because we had to draw a still life while shading with cross-hatching methods.

OTHER WORKS

3D Can House

Atmospheric Drawing

These 3D projects were made using Cinema 4D, Autodesk Maya and Zbrush, in that order. The spear was a practice piece so that I could understand Maya's set up, and then I moved on to creating other pieces like the can house. The can was rendered using Arnold in Maya. The last piece on the right was a background I drew in Photoshop for an animation I was making to practice perspective.

ABOUT ME

Hi, I am Danielly Zurita Torres and I'm a student at Mercy University studying Design and Animation. I have been a student there for almost 4 years and have taken a handful of classes centered to develop my skills. I have worked on various design projects and made a few animation shorts and tests.

I've also been learning more 3D modeling during my free time. And occasionally I make traditional art out of acrylic or watercolor paints. Recently I have started to work with 3D animation using Cinema4D.

I'm excited to work on more projects in the future.
